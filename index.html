About Me

I've always been fascinated by machines that think, adapt, and solve problems.

That curiosity pushed me from mechanical engineering into robotics — where hardware, software, and intelligence collide. I've built microbial fuel cells that give robots bio-inspired emotions, and cross-domain localization tested on land, air, and sea.

I've worked on quadrupeds that learn to walk, agentic AI workflows, and vision-guided teleoperation. Today I focus on systems that don't just function — they leave a mark.
